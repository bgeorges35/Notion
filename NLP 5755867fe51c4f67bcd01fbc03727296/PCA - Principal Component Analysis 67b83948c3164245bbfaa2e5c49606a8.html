PCA - Principal Component Analysis

An algorithm to reduce the number of dimension of a data (while trying to keep as much information as possible)

Useful to make representation of data that have a lot of dimension

Terminology

  • Eigenvector → "Singular Vector" == "Unit Vector"
  • Loading Scores →
  • Eigenvalue → sum of square distances for the best lines
  • visual
  • Singular value → square root of the Eighenvalue
  • Scree plot → Graphical representation of the percentage of variation of each PC
  • PC → Principal component
  • visual

Steps

  1. Mean normalise data "Center data"
  1. Get covariance matrix
  1. Perform SVD (Singular Variance Decomposition)

    ⇒ It give us 3 matrices:

    • The first (U) has the Eigenvectors in the column
    • The second (S) one has the Eigenvalues in the diagonal

  1. Dot product between

    if you want 2 dimension at the end (which is common)

  1. Percentage of variance retained

StatQuest steps:

  1. Center the data (by subtracting the mean)
  1. Find best fitting line that goes through the origin (PC1) using the sum of square distances for the best lines (or Eigenvalues)
  1. Find the others PC (best fitting line that goes through the origin and is perpendicular to the other PC One by dimension
  1. With all our Eigenvalues, determine the important feature (with a scree plot for exemple)
  1. Cast the data on the chosen features
